KEY NEARBY TEMPLES & PILGRIMAGE SITES:

1. Kondagattu Sri Anjaneya Swamy Temple (closest, ~6-7 km):
   - Famous 300-year-old hilltop Hanuman temple in Muthyampet village, Mallial mandal.
   - The main deity is a Swayambhu (self-manifested) idol of Lord Anjaneya with a Narasimha face.
   - Devotees from all over Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, and Maharashtra visit.
   - Known for the 41-day Hanuman Deeksha tradition — childless couples offer 40-day prayers here.
   - Hanuman Jayanti festival draws over 4 lakh devotees.
   - Temple timings: 4 AM to 8:30 PM.
   - Also nearby: Kondalaraya Fort and Bojjapotana caves.

2. Vemulawada Sri Raja Rajeshwara Swamy Temple (Rajanna Temple, ~24 km):
   - One of the most important Shiva temples in South India, known as "Dakshina Kasi".
   - 9th-10th century Chalukya architecture — dedicated to Lord Shiva as Neela Lohitha Siva Lingam.
   - Also called "Harihara Kshetram" — both Shiva and Vishnu shrines within the complex.
   - Unique symbol of communal harmony — also houses a Muslim saint's tomb.
   - Sacred Dharma Gundam tank with medicinal properties.
   - Famous tradition: pulling the bull around the temple for wish fulfillment.
   - Maha Shivratri and Kartik Month are the biggest festivals.

3. Dharmapuri Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple (~47 km):
   - One of the Nava Narasimha Kshetrams (nine major Narasimha shrines in India).
   - Located on the southern bank of Godavari River where it flows southward (Dakshinavahini).
   - Unique: three forms of Narasimha — Yoga Narasimha, Jwala Narasimha, and Gandabherunda Narasimha.
   - Over 800 years old — inscriptions date to 1018 AD.
   - Godavari Pushkaralu next in 2027 — major festival.
   - Known as one of the biggest settlements of Vedic Brahmins in Telangana with a Sanskrit college.
   - Nearby: ancient Ramalingeswara Temple (~1000 years old).
   - Temple timings: 5 AM to 8 PM (break 2:30-4 PM).

These temples are popular pilgrimage circuits — many devotees visit all three (Kondagattu → Vemulawada → Dharmapuri) in one trip.
